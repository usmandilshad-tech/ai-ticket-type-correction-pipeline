# Escalation Policy

## When to Escalate

A support ticket should be escalated when first-level support cannot resolve the issue, when approval is required, or when the issue has business, security, financial, or technical risk.

## Escalate to Billing Team

Escalate to billing if:

1. The customer reports duplicate completed charges.
2. A refund requires approval.
3. The invoice is incorrect.
4. Payment status cannot be confirmed.
5. The customer reports a financial dispute.

## Escalate to Technical Team

Escalate to technical support or engineering if:

1. The customer reports a reproducible bug.
2. The issue causes data loss.
3. The issue affects login or account access after normal troubleshooting.
4. The issue affects multiple users.
5. The customer provides error logs, screenshots, or technical details.

## Escalate to Customer Success or Management

Escalate to customer success or management if:

1. The customer is highly dissatisfied.
2. The customer threatens cancellation due to unresolved issues.
3. The customer is a high-value account.
4. The issue has remained unresolved for too long.

## Escalation Note Format

When escalating, include:

1. Customer name or account ID.
2. Ticket ID.
3. Issue summary.
4. Steps already taken.
5. Screenshots, logs, or evidence if available.
6. Requested action.
7. Priority level.
